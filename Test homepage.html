December, 30

Two more days until the new year is here and I am already anxiously thrilled! My passport has finally arrived and while I've been told it is weird, I find it absolutely adorable. Possibly because it brings promises of freedom, adventure, and lots and lots and lots of food.... Either way, we are just two weeks away from the first trip: Seattle! Yes, a US city, not very exciting but this will be my first trip traveling on my own. My boyfriend, Ander and I will travel together, but I will travel by myself on the way back. EEK! What could possibly go wrong? Well, let's see: I loose my tickets, get lost in the airport, grab the wrong plane, never make it to the gate, be abducted by crazy Seattle kidnappers....my brain seriously does not shut up. Either way, we are two weeks away from coffee, rainy weather, and a ferry. Yes, a ferry! Did I mention I've never been on a boat before either?? On top of prepping for trip number one, dates have been finalized for Poland! The trip that started this all will be my last trip for 2020; so to accomplish final dates and bookings is a huge check in my book. I will be traveling on November 20th to the 30th (flight days, yay.) and am incredibly excited to explore six different cities. Oh course, what do I choose to with my time now? Look at how I can finagle my work schedule to squeeze in tiny summer trips; as if I am not travelling enough for the new year! Like seriously, a weekend in Paris, Amsterdam, Prague, Barcelona...I can't decide! I want to do a four day trip the weekend of July 4th (cause who doesn't want to spend the American Independence Day in another country?!). My hope is to find a trip in which I fly out Thursday evening (July 2nd) and return Monday afternoon (July 6th).

December, 24

A little about me as I continue packing and planning. I am a working student with a huge passion in literature and history. I've been "working" on a double major in Politics and History for a while now (I say "working" because I've had to walk away from a lot of schooling due to mental health issues), but I've felt something missing from my studies. The first adventure I have planned is a short, weekend trip to Seattle. Yes, that doesn't compare to the other trips planned for the rest of the year but the important item is baby steps! I struggle with a lot of anxiety. Hell, I had an anxiety attack and complete meltdown in the passport agency parking lot over a grumpy boomer! And I'm expecting myself to be fine abroad?! Fuck no! Here's the thing, my goal with this blog is to not only track my attitude and wonders along the way, cause hey, its really meant for me right? But also share what it is like to travel with anxiety and any tips and tricks I find along the way. So are we ready? No, but let's go!
